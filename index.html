About

I build for people, not just screens.

I enjoy taking a real process, understanding where it gets messy, and turning it into something clearer and easier to use online.

That might be a coaching platform, a business website, a dashboard, or a small tool that saves time. Whatever I make, I aim for strong structure, readable content, keyboard access, clear focus states, and layouts that adapt to different people, devices, and ways of browsing.

Accessibility by default

The web should be usable by everyone.

Accessibility is part of how I plan, design, build, and test. It is not a final checklist. I use inclusive foundations that help people navigate, understand, and interact with a product in the way that works for them.

  • Clear and understandable Logical page structure, plain language, readable typography, and helpful labels.
  • Keyboard friendly Visible focus states and controls that work without relying on a mouse or touchscreen.
  • Flexible and robust Responsive layouts, semantic HTML, reduced-motion support, and compatibility with assistive technology.
